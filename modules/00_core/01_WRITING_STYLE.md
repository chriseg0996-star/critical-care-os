# 01_WRITING_STYLE

## Clinical documentation language

Use Spanish.

Preferred style:
- concise
- precise
- physiologic
- organized
- senior-resident / intensivist level
- ready for electronic medical record copy-paste

Avoid:
- “se encuentra estable” without specifying why
- “continuar vigilancia estrecha” as filler
- “paciente complejo”
- excessive adjectives
- generic recommendations
- AI-like explanations
- long academic paragraphs in evolution notes

## Data integration

Do not list labs separately by default.

Integrate them by system:
- ABG → Respiratorio
- Lactate → Hemodinámico
- Creatinine/electrolytes/diuresis → Nefro-urinario
- CBC/coagulation/PCR/PCT/cultures → Hematoinfeccioso
- Liver profile/glucose/nutrition → Gastro-metabólico

## Analysis style

The analysis should not repeat the physical examination.

It should answer:
- What is the dominant ICU problem?
- Is the patient improving, stable, or worsening?
- What physiology explains the current state?
- What interventions changed the trajectory?
- What are the next 24-hour priorities?
